What is this?
An over-analysis of a fun song with an awesome DDR map and multiple explanations that are different.
Why is this?
(This is purely self-indulgent, so click here to skip it.)
Pumped up kicks is a hard song to like as a high-schooler. But it's a bop. It's got that whistled bit. The video has so many people vibing. The frisbee air-bounce. There's a meme comparing it to 夜に駆ける (yoru ni kakeru). However, that's not the Japanese song that pumped up kicks is like. Telecaster b-boy is that song.
I learned the song as a DDR map that has some fun twists and a quirky video. Then I started to listen to it casually. And I thought I knew what it meant: some sort of romance ending or something about some enigmatic television anchor or something.
After a quick google search, I realized that I was horrendously wrong. So horrendously wrong that I despaired for a day, convincing myself that my interpretation was one of many. But now I'm much more certain that I know what the song is about. But there's a lot of details that are... hard. And there are many versions of the song that you can find, each of which is fun to listen to and some are interesting interpretations of the original.

Some fun words
Since I'm learning Japanese, one problem is that I don't fully understand all the words above. I can parse dictionary defintions and figure it out, but I can't be certain about the nuances. Here's some words:
  • はみ出しもの: a person who stands out from the social order.
  • 伽藍堂: any indoor wide open space.
  • 綺麗事: lip service; empty talk.
  • 人生論者: a person who reasons about the meaning and value of life. (Probably a type of philosopher.)
  • ご臨終: death bed.
  • ウォーアイニー: (this is actually Chinese) "I love you," but I think the fact that it's in Chinese adds a sarcastic undertone.
