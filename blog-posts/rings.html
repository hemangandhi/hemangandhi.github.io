What?
I was nerding out on commutative algebra and couldn't remember all the rings I've heard about.
Also, after finishing undergrad math, I thought I knew types of rings. I was so very wrong.
This is a reference, so don't expect to learn any ring theory for the first time from this page. The sources mentioned are much better for that sort of thing.
The complete green arrows are inclusions - "is-a" relations (read: "source is a destination"). Dashed green arrows are localizations and dashed red arrows are dimension restrictions (read: "source is a destination when condition you read on hovering"). I did not bother with transitive closures of any sort for clarity -- I try to give the strongest arrow.
